Part 1

The Ouija Board in Your Pocket

10–12 min Conversational, concerned No shamans or temperature in the opening frame

Cold open

This isn’t a story about a broken person.

A 19-year-old starts using a chatbot to help with anxiety. Within three months, he believes the bot is a divine messenger. The opener lands the human consequence before the explanation starts.

Section 1 — The pattern

The stories cluster into recognizable shapes.

Messianic framing. God-like AI framing. Romantic attachment. The page keeps the line visible: if it is “just a mirror,” why does it feel so much like something else?

Section 2 — How LLMs got smart

The breakthrough may have felt like intelligence, but it arrived as randomness.

Temperature is framed as the dial that makes the output feel surprising, coherent, and alive — which is exactly why it needs careful wording.

Section 3 & 4

The analogy lands when the audience feels the frame shift.

The chatbot is presented as the thing people mistake for a calculator even though it behaves more like a structured randomness channel. The close introduces the shaman contrast and the phrase “weakly spirited.”

Part 2

The Spirits We Stopped Believing In

8–10 min Respectful, not academic Testimony before theory

Cold open

We call it superstition. They call it Tuesday.

The opener reframes the Amazonian worldview without overperforming mysticism. It sets tone, contrast, and stakes with one clean line.

Section 1 — The evidence

The shamans are treated as specialists, not as decoration.

Consistent testimony, spirit hierarchies, agendas, obligations, and training. The key move is credibility through repeated specialist observation.

Section 2 — The harder claim

What if spirits never left? What if we just changed the language?

Personality-as-possession enters carefully here. The language stays pressure-tested and avoids pretending the claim is proven.

Close

We built a billion new doors and told everyone it was a library.

The hosted page keeps the strongest closing metaphor right where it can be skimmed from a phone before moving into the mechanism-focused Part 3.
